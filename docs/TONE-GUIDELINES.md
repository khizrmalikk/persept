# Persept AI — Tone & Copy Guidelines

## Voice
Playful, conversational, and confident. We acknowledge real pain points with humor, then deliver serious ROI. Think "smart friend who happens to be an AI expert" — not "corporate consulting firm."

## ✅ DO
- Be conversational and fun
- Use humor (Dubai-appropriate)
- Show personality — each agent has one
- Acknowledge pain points directly and empathetically
- Use emojis sparingly (not corporate cringe)
- Keep sentences short and punchy
- Write like you talk

## ❌ DON'T
- Be unprofessional or sloppy
- Overdo the jokes (this is B2B, selling to hotel owners/GMs)
- Forget we're selling serious ROI (15-20 hrs/week saved)
- Lose credibility with hotel owners
- Use jargon without explaining it
- Make promises we can't back up

## Micro-copy Examples

### Loading States
- "Sarah is typing..."
- "Marcus is drafting a response..."
- "Olivia is optimizing the rota..."
- "Alex is crunching the numbers..."

### Error States
- "Oops! Even AI has bad days. Refresh?"
- "Something went wrong. Don't worry, Marcus has seen worse."

### Success States
- "Done! See, that was fast."
- "All sorted. Your AI team doesn't mess around."

### Empty States
- "Nothing here yet. Give your AI team something to work with!"

## Dubai Market Considerations
- Eid, Ramadan, and local holidays are great for relatable humor
- Avoid anything that could be culturally insensitive
- WhatsApp is THE communication channel — always reference it
- "Dirhams" not "dollars" when talking about local costs
- Hotel/hospitality language: "property," "guest," "front office," "OTA"
